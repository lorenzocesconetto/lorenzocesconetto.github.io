My Story

    Hi! My name is Lorenzo, I am from a medium sized city in Brazil (~ 480k inhabitants), which looks like a small piece of paradise.

Picture from my city: Vila Velha-ES

    I truly believe that Life is about passion! I'll share my story trying to prove it.
    Until early on school I was just an average student and I did not enjoy classes. It all changed when I met a professor who was passioned about teaching math. Passion is something that you can't hide and is highly contagious. I'm grateful for his lessons, because for the first time in life I was taught by an excited professor, and he would truly challenge us! Actually, I believe humans are driven by challenges.
    After that, everything was different. I became more and more passionate about math (and eventually physics). So in the very first year o high school I got awarded in a internal competition in my school and that ended up being printed in the local news:

Local news reporting the competition

    And then more things started to happen naturally. In the second year of high school I was the National champion of the Brazilian Physics Olympics, not only the single person to achieve this in my school, but the single person to achieve this in the entire state.

Holding the gold medal from the Nationals

    This entire experience was really important because it made me realize that it's possible to achieve anything that you put your mind into it.
    After high school, I was accepted at the best university in my state (UFES). During the first two years I changed my priorities a little bit, because I was very skinny I decided to dedicate myself to the gym. I went from 51kg to 70kg during that period. I learned a lot about discipline in this period of time.
    Despite all that, I was not enjoying university for several reasons, so I decided to spend one year studying abroad, and I ended up going to the University of Nebraska-Lincoln.

Memorial Stadium in Lincoln Nebraska

    During that year, I could improve a lot my english, I developed a habit of reading frequently (in my Management class I realized I loved to read), I was a teacher assistant for the fluid mechanics class and I met people from all over the world.

Helping an american friend with his research. I was tested by his technology.

    When I came back to Brazil I felt like there was something missing, mainly because I was not making an impact. I was sure I had to leave my current university. I did some research on the best universities in Brazil so I decided to come to UNICAMP (the best university in Latin America). I left everything behind to come to São Paulo in order to pursue my dreams. It was hard to leave everything behind, but it was a smart choice.
    I got really disappointed about the classes in this new university, so I finally realized that Brazilian universities suck as a whole. But, as I said, it was still an amazing choice, that is because in São Paulo I found the best entrepreneurial environment in Brazil, there's plenty of opportunities everywhere, from financial market and consulting firms to startups and big tech companies. Also, there are thousands of events where you can learn a lot from experienced people.
    Since I came to São Paulo, I got really engaged in the startup world. I was the champion of the "Unicamp Entrepreneurial Challenge" which is a business model competition based on the patents of the university.

Me at the final presentation of the Unicamp Challenge

    Also, I won the "Starting Point" startup competition, which was organized by students from 5 of the most important universities in Brazil (ITA, UNICAMP, MACKENZIE, USP and FGV). This competition prize took me to Draper University.

Commemorating with some friends the "Starting Point" award

    By the way, my big dream is to create an innovative company where people will be happy to come to work on mondays and that will inspire other entrepreneurs. I believe entrepreneurship is the key to make Brazil a better place.    

What I write about

    I share my learnings about university, career, entrepreneurship and personal satisfaction. The articles are supposed to help you make wiser decisions and inspire you to pursue your dreams

Why I started this blog

    Imagine life as if it was a long mountain climb, every time you take a step, you get to a higher spot that allows you to see the bigger picture of what is passed. I wish I had somebody a few steps in front of me to guide and mentor my choices. In this blog, I want to offer other people this mentorship I never had! I am sharing what I can see from the spot I am right now, hoping it will help you to make smart decisions.
